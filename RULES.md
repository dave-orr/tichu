# Tichu — Complete Rules Reference

## Overview
Tichu is a partnership climbing card game for exactly 4 players. Players sit in a circle; partners sit across from each other. The goal is to be the first team to reach 1000 points across multiple rounds.

## The Deck (56 cards)
- **Standard cards (52):** Four suits (Jade, Sword, Pagoda, Star), each with ranks 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, Jack, Queen, King, Ace.
- **Special cards (4):**
  - **Mah Jong** (the 1): The lowest-ranked card.
  - **Dog** (the Hound): No rank, no trick value.
  - **Phoenix**: Wild card, worth −25 points.
  - **Dragon**: Highest single card, worth +25 points.

## Card Point Values
| Card | Points |
|------|--------|
| 5s | 5 each |
| 10s | 10 each |
| Kings | 10 each |
| Dragon | 25 |
| Phoenix | −25 |
| All others | 0 |

The total point value of the entire deck is always **100 points**.

## Dealing
1. All 56 cards are dealt evenly — **14 cards per player**.
2. Cards are dealt in two stages:
   - First 8 cards dealt to each player.
   - Players may declare **Grand Tichu** after seeing these 8 cards.
   - Remaining 6 cards dealt to each player.

## Tichu Calls
- **Grand Tichu**: May be declared after seeing your first 8 cards but **before** receiving the remaining 6. Worth **+200 points** if the caller goes out first, **−200** otherwise.
- **Small Tichu**: May be declared at any time **before playing your first card** of the round. Worth **+100 points** if the caller goes out first, **−100** otherwise.
- Multiple players (even partners) may call Tichu in the same round. Only one can succeed.

## Card Passing
After all 14 cards are dealt (and Grand Tichu window closes):
- Each player selects **3 cards** from their hand.
- One card is passed to each of the other three players (one to partner, one to each opponent).
- All passes happen simultaneously — you pass before seeing what you receive.

## Card Combinations (Legal Plays)
1. **Single card**: Any one card.
2. **Pair**: Two cards of the same rank.
3. **Consecutive pairs** (stairs): Two or more pairs with consecutive ranks (e.g., 5-5-6-6 or 8-8-9-9-10-10). Minimum 2 pairs.
4. **Triple**: Three cards of the same rank.
5. **Full house**: A triple plus a pair. Ranked by the triple.
6. **Straight**: Five or more cards with consecutive ranks, regardless of suit. Aces are high (no wrap-around).
7. **Four-of-a-kind bomb**: All four cards of one rank. Beats any non-bomb combination.
8. **Straight flush bomb**: Five or more consecutive cards of the **same suit**. Beats four-of-a-kind bombs. Longer straight flushes beat shorter ones; same-length ones are compared by highest card.

## Playing the Game

### Starting a Trick
- The player holding the **Mah Jong** leads the first trick of the round.
- After a trick is won, the winner leads the next trick.
- If the winner has no cards left, the lead passes to the **right** (next in turn order).

### Following
- Play proceeds to the **right** (counter-clockwise).
- A player may **pass** or play a combination that:
  - Is the **same type** as the led combination.
  - Has a **higher rank** than the current top combination.
  - Has the **same length** (for straights and consecutive pairs).
- Passing does not prevent you from playing later in the same trick.

### Winning a Trick
- When **three consecutive players pass** after the last play, the player who made that last play wins the trick.
- The trick winner collects all cards in the trick (for scoring) and leads the next trick.

## Bombs
- A **bomb** can be played **at any time**, including **out of turn**.
- Bombs beat any non-bomb combination and can interrupt any trick.
- A bomb can only be beaten by a **higher bomb**:
  - A straight flush bomb beats a four-of-a-kind bomb.
  - A longer straight flush beats a shorter one.
  - Between same-length straight flushes, the higher-ranked one wins.
  - A higher four-of-a-kind beats a lower four-of-a-kind.
- After a bomb is played, all players get a chance to play a higher bomb before the trick resolves.

## Special Card Rules

### Mah Jong (1)
- Has a rank of **1** — the lowest card.
- Can be part of a straight (e.g., 1-2-3-4-5).
- When played (in any combination), the player makes a **wish**: they name a specific rank (2 through Ace).
- The wish persists for the rest of the trick and subsequent tricks until fulfilled.
- Any player who can legally play the wished rank **must** do so (but they don't have to play it in a losing combination — they only must include it if they can make a legal play containing it).
- The wish is canceled once any player plays a card of the wished rank.

### Dog (Hound)
- Can **only** be played as a single card when you have the **lead** (start of a trick).
- It has **no trick value** and cannot be beaten.
- It immediately passes the lead to your **partner**.
- If your partner is already out, the lead passes to the next active player to the right.
- The Dog cannot be bombed.

### Phoenix
- Worth **−25 points** in scoring.
- As a **single card**: Its value is **0.5 higher** than the last single card played. If led, it counts as rank 1.5. It can beat an Ace but **cannot beat the Dragon**.
- In **combinations**: Acts as a **wild card** — it can substitute for any normal card to form a pair, straight, full house, triple, or consecutive pairs.
- **Cannot be used in a bomb** (no wild bombs allowed).

### Dragon
- The **highest single card** in the game. Worth **+25 points**.
- Can only be played as a **single card** — never in combinations.
- Can only be beaten by a **bomb**.
- If the Dragon **wins a trick**, the player who played the Dragon must **give the entire trick** (including the Dragon's 25 points) to one of the **two opponents** (player's choice).

## End of a Round
- The round ends when **3 of 4 players** have played all their cards.
- The last player still holding cards:
  - Gives all remaining **hand cards** to the **opposing team**.
  - Gives all **tricks they won** to the player who went out **first**.

### 1-2 Finish (Double Victory)
- If **both partners** go out **1st and 2nd** (before either opponent), their team scores **200 points** and no card points are counted for that round.
- Tichu bonuses/penalties still apply on top of this.

## Scoring a Round
1. If a 1-2 finish occurred: that team gets **200 points**; the other team gets **0 card points**.
2. Otherwise: each team counts the card points in their collected tricks.
   - Last player's hand cards go to opponents.
   - Last player's tricks go to the first-out player.
3. Add/subtract Tichu bonuses:
   - Grand Tichu: ±200
   - Small Tichu: ±100
4. Add to cumulative team scores.

## Winning the Game
- The game ends at the conclusion of any round where **at least one team has 1000+ points**.
- The team with the **higher score wins**.
- If tied at 1000+, play another round until the tie is broken.
